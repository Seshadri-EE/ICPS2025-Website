About ICPS 2025

The International Conference on Power Systems (ICPS) is a leading forum in the field of Power Engineering, continuing a biennial tradition that began in 2004. Conceived by academicians from India and Nepal, ICPS brings together experts from academia, industry, research, and practice to explore the latest advancements and innovations across various domains of power systems.

The 11th International Conference on Power Systems (ICPS 2025), as part of this esteemed series, will take place from 7th - 9th December 2025 at the Department of Electrical Engineering, Indian Institute of Technology Hyderabad (IIT Hyderabad), India.

About the Conference Organizer

The Indian Institute of Technology Hyderabad (IITH), established in 2008, is a premier institution recognized for its strong emphasis on advanced teaching, research, and innovation. Consistently ranked among the top 10 engineering schools in India by NIRF, IITH provides a dynamic academic environment where cutting-edge research seamlessly integrates with world-class teaching. The institute encourages interdisciplinary collaboration, offering students a well-rounded education that combines deep theoretical insights with practical, real-world applications. With state-of-the-art infrastructure and strong partnerships with industry and global academic institutions, IITH is at the forefront of addressing contemporary technological challenges and driving impactful solutions in various fields.

The Department of Electrical Engineering (EE) at IITH plays a pivotal role in this mission, offering both undergraduate and postgraduate programs in a variety of electrical engineering disciplines. The department is home to a team of highly qualified faculty, researchers, and students who are engaged in pioneering research and development. It is structured into several specialized verticals to foster focused and impactful research: Communications, Signal Processing, and Learning (CSPL); Microelectronics and VLSI (Micro); Nanodevices and Technology; Integrated Circuits and Systems; Power Electronics and Power Systems (PEPS); and Systems and Control (SysCon). These verticals allow the department to address a broad spectrum of emerging challenges in electrical engineering, including smart grids, power systems, microelectronics, and communications. Through its research excellence and academic programs, the EE department at IITH is making significant contributions to the advancement of electrical engineering and technology.
